About Me

I am Nawab Amedi, a second-year Software Engineering student at the University of Ottawa. I am interested in software development, technology, and design, and I enjoy learning new skills through both coursework and personal projects. I first became interested in programming through gaming and technology, which eventually led me to build my own computer and learn more about how software works behind the scenes. Outside of school, I enjoy playing sports, spending time with friends, and playing video games. These interests have helped me build problem-solving skills, teamwork, patience, and the ability to think quickly under pressure. I like working on projects that let me be creative while also challenging me to think logically and improve my technical skills over time.

How I Work

I do not currently have professional experience in UI design, but I am currently taking a UI design course where I am learning the fundamentals of interface design, including layout, accessibility, color theory, and user-centered design principles. I also use resources such as the Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g) to learn more about usability standards and how professional designers approach different problems.

When I work on projects, I usually like to start by understanding the overall goal first before jumping into the design itself. I prefer keeping things organized and simple in the beginning so I can build on ideas step by step instead of trying to do everything at once. I normally experiment with different layouts and designs, see what works best, and then make changes based on feedback or things I notice while working.

I learn best through practice, so a lot of my process involves trying things out, making mistakes, and improving from there. I also pay attention to small details because I think even simple changes can make a design feel much cleaner and easier to use. As I continue through the course, I will keep building my understanding of concepts such as heuristic evaluation, accessibility, iterative design, and user-centered design while improving my overall workflow and design process.
